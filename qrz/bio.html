Good morning from Louisville

Hi, I'm Eric - W4EWB, on the air from Louisville, Kentucky (grid EM78). Easy to remember with Eggs, Waffles and Bacon.

After working at RadioShack through high school in the early 2000s, I’m proud to have finally earned my General Class license. I made it halfway through the CS enginerring program at UKY, before I discovered I like people more than machines, so I changed course to become a psychotherapist. I've found ham radio to be the perfect marraige of two. I’m a father of two, a psychotherapist by day, and a tinkerer/programmer in my spare time. My wife and I love visiting family out in Utah whenever we can.

I love wiring the shack up so it half-runs itself. Most nights you'll find me chasing digital DX, sending pictures over the air, or writing yet another little program to make the station do something clever.

The Station
The corner shack; FT-710, a mini pc and mini mac
Wire in the trees out back simple, quiet, and it gets out.

Rig: Yaesu FT-710.   Antenna: The Buckmaster 7 Band OFC.   Modes: I live on the digital end - VarAC and FT8 for keyboard-to-keyboard DX, SSTV for trading pictures, and lately I've been learning my way around CW. If you hear me, please come back a new grid still makes my whole day.

What I Build

Half the fun for me is the workbench behind the radio. A few things I've put together for the W4EWB station:

  • A station control center - one dashboard that logs my contacts and pushes them to LoTW, QRZ and World Radio League, watches the bands, and keeps an eye on everything running in the shack.
  • A HamClock-style wall display - a live world map with grayline, space-weather, the sun and moon, DX spots and beacons, running on a spare monitor.
  • Auto-publishing stations -; my SSTV receiver and FT8 monitor post what they hear to the web automatically (have a look below).
  • A few experiments — including letting an AI assistant lend a hand around the shack. Ham radio has always been about tinkering; this is just my flavor of it.
